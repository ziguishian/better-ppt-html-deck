# Slide Patterns

Use these page types to map content to visual structure.

## cover

- Best for: title, promise, event/report identity.
- Layout: large title, short subtitle, hero visual, minimal metadata.
- Focus: instant impact.
- Avoid: dense agenda text.
- Components: SlideFrame, EditableText, EditableImage, IconBadge.

## section

- Best for: chapter breaks.
- Layout: section number, short heading, transition visual.
- Focus: reset attention.
- Avoid: detailed explanations.
- Components: NumberCard, QuoteBlock.

## big-idea

- Best for: one key claim.
- Layout: oversized statement with one supporting line.
- Focus: memorable message.
- Avoid: lists and multiple claims.
- Components: EditableText, FloatingPanel.

## two-column

- Best for: explanation plus evidence, text plus visual.
- Layout: dominant column and support column.
- Focus: balanced comparison of idea and proof.
- Avoid: two equal dense text blocks.
- Components: GlassCard, EditableImage, BrowserFrame.

## comparison

- Best for: before/after, A/B, old/new.
- Layout: two or three panels with aligned criteria.
- Focus: contrast.
- Avoid: unrelated dimensions.
- Components: ComparisonDiagram, NumberCard.

## timeline

- Best for: roadmap, history, implementation phases.
- Layout: horizontal or vertical sequence.
- Focus: clear chronology.
- Avoid: too many milestones.
- Components: TimelineDiagram.

## process

- Best for: workflows, steps, pipelines.
- Layout: connected stages.
- Focus: direction and dependencies.
- Avoid: cyclic systems without loops.
- Components: ProcessDiagram, PipelineDiagram.

## architecture

- Best for: systems, agents, products, platforms.
- Layout: layers, nodes, flows.
- Focus: relationships and responsibilities.
- Avoid: decorative pseudo-architecture.
- Components: ArchitectureDiagram, AgentLoopDiagram.

## quote

- Best for: customer voice, principle, memorable line.
- Layout: large quote, attribution, optional context.
- Focus: emotional or strategic emphasis.
- Avoid: long paragraphs.
- Components: QuoteBlock.

## case-study

- Best for: concrete example.
- Layout: context, action, result.
- Focus: proof.
- Avoid: vague success claims.
- Components: BrowserFrame, DataCard.

## tutorial-step

- Best for: teaching a sequence.
- Layout: step number, action, screenshot/code/result.
- Focus: learnable action.
- Avoid: multiple unrelated actions.
- Components: CodeBlock, TerminalWindow, FileTree.

## code-demo

- Best for: explaining code.
- Layout: code block plus annotations.
- Focus: what changed and why.
- Avoid: full files with tiny text.
- Components: CodeBlock, FileTree.

## terminal-demo

- Best for: commands and outputs.
- Layout: terminal frame with short command/output.
- Focus: reproducible action.
- Avoid: long logs.
- Components: TerminalWindow, TerminalCommandBlock.

## data-dashboard

- Best for: metrics, KPIs, status.
- Layout: few large metrics plus chart/table.
- Focus: signal, not decoration.
- Avoid: many tiny metrics.
- Components: NumberCard, DataCard, ComparisonDiagram.

## checklist

- Best for: criteria, requirements, readiness.
- Layout: grouped checks.
- Focus: scannability.
- Avoid: long prose.
- Components: IconBadge, GlassCard.

## summary

- Best for: recap and takeaways.
- Layout: 3-5 key points.
- Focus: memory.
- Avoid: repeating every detail.
- Components: NumberCard, QuoteBlock.

## closing

- Best for: final message, CTA, Q&A.
- Layout: strong statement, contact or next step.
- Focus: clean ending.
- Avoid: new content.
- Components: EditableText, EditableImage.
